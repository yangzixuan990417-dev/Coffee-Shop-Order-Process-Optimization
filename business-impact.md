# Business Impact and KPI Analysis

## 1. Purpose

This document explains the expected business impact of the redesigned To-Be coffee shop order process.

The purpose of the To-Be process is not only to improve the visual workflow, but also to create a more efficient, accurate, and system-supported order process. The redesigned process introduces real-time inventory checking, structured POS order capture, automatic inventory deduction, low-stock alerts, and reorder request generation.

These improvements help reduce manual work, improve stock visibility, and support better operational decision-making.

---

## 2. Key Business Problems in the As-Is Process

The original As-Is process had several operational weaknesses:

- Inventory availability was checked too late in the process.
- Staff relied heavily on manual communication.
- Customers could place orders for unavailable items.
- Inventory updates were delayed or handled manually.
- Managers had limited real-time visibility over stock levels.
- Reordering was reactive rather than proactive.

These problems could lead to order delays, customer dissatisfaction, stockouts, and inaccurate inventory records.

---

## 3. Expected Business Impact

| Business Area | As-Is Issue | To-Be Improvement | Expected Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Order accuracy | Order details may be handled manually or inconsistently. | POS captures structured order details. | Reduces order entry errors and improves traceability. |
| Inventory visibility | Stock levels are not updated in real time. | Inventory is automatically deducted after each transaction. | Provides more accurate and timely stock information. |
| Customer experience | Customers may only learn that an item is unavailable after ordering. | Inventory is checked before order confirmation. | Reduces customer frustration and failed orders. |
| Exception handling | Out-of-stock situations depend on staff explanation. | POS displays unavailable items and recommends alternatives. | Makes the process faster and more consistent. |
| Barista workflow | Orders may be communicated manually. | Confirmed orders are sent directly to the barista. | Reduces miscommunication and improves preparation flow. |
| Reorder management | Managers identify low stock manually. | System generates low-stock alerts and reorder requests. | Supports proactive inventory replenishment. |
| Decision-making | Managers rely on observation and experience. | System provides structured order and inventory data. | Enables more data-driven operational decisions. |

## 4. KPI Framework

The following KPIs can be used to evaluate whether the redesigned process improves business performance.

| KPI | What It Measures | As-Is Situation | To-Be Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Order error rate | Percentage of orders with incorrect or missing information. | Higher risk due to manual order handling. | Reduce errors through structured POS capture. |
| Stockout detection time | Time taken to identify unavailable items. | Detected late during preparation or manual checking. | Detected immediately through real-time inventory checks. |
| Inventory update frequency | How often stock records are updated. | Updated manually or at the end of the day. | Updated automatically after every completed transaction. |
| Out-of-stock resolution rate | Percentage of out-of-stock cases resolved by alternative item selection. | Depends on staff communication. | Improved through system-recommended alternatives. |
| Reorder response time | Time between low stock occurrence and reorder action. | Delayed because managers must identify issues manually. | Reduced through automated low-stock alerts. |
| Order completion rate | Percentage of orders successfully completed after being placed. | Lower if unavailable items are discovered late. | Improved because availability is checked before payment. |
| Customer waiting uncertainty | Whether customers know when their order is ready. | Customer waits for staff to manually call the order. | Reduced through order-ready notification. |

## 6. Business Value Summary

The redesigned To-Be process creates business value in three main ways.

First, it improves operational efficiency by reducing manual communication between the cashier, barista, inventory system, and manager. Orders are captured in a structured way and passed through the system more clearly.

Second, it improves inventory control by checking availability before order confirmation and automatically updating stock levels after each transaction. This reduces the risk of selling unavailable items and helps managers respond to low-stock situations earlier.

Third, it improves customer experience by preventing failed orders, recommending available alternatives, and notifying customers when their order is ready.

Overall, the To-Be process turns the coffee shop order workflow from a reactive, manual process into a more proactive, system-supported process.
