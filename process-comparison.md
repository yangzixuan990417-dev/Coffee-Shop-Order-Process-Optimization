# Process Comparison

## As-Is vs To-Be Process Comparison

| Area | As-Is Process | Problem Identified | To-Be Process Improvement | Expected Business Impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Order capture | Customer places an order and staff manually handle the order details. | Order information may be incomplete, inconsistent, or delayed. | POS captures structured order details immediately after the customer places an order. | Reduces order errors and improves order traceability. |
| Inventory checking | Item availability is checked manually or discovered late during order preparation. | Customers may only find out an item is unavailable after ordering or waiting. | Inventory system performs a real-time inventory check before the order is confirmed. | Prevents unavailable items from being confirmed and improves customer experience. |
| Out-of-stock handling | Staff manually explain unavailable items and customers may need to restart the ordering process. | Handling depends on staff memory and may cause confusion or delays. | POS displays an out-of-stock message and recommends available alternatives. | Makes exception handling clearer, faster, and more consistent. |
| Order confirmation | Staff confirm the order after checking availability manually. | Confirmation may happen before stock availability is fully validated. | Order is confirmed only after the inventory system verifies that the item is available. | Improves process reliability and reduces failed orders. |
| Payment process | Customer payment may occur before stock availability is clearly confirmed. | Refunds or corrections may be needed if the item is unavailable. | Payment happens after order confirmation. | Reduces unnecessary refunds and improves transaction accuracy. |
| Transaction recording | Sales are recorded after payment, but may not be connected to inventory updates. | Sales and inventory data may become inconsistent. | POS records the transaction and triggers automatic inventory deduction. | Improves data accuracy across sales and inventory records. |
| Inventory update | Stock levels are checked or updated manually after service or at the end of the day. | Inventory data is delayed and may not reflect actual stock levels. | Inventory is automatically deducted after each completed transaction. | Enables more accurate real-time stock visibility. |
| Barista workflow | Barista receives order information through staff communication. | Orders may be miscommunicated or delayed during busy periods. | POS sends confirmed orders directly to the barista. | Improves order handoff and reduces communication errors. |
| Order-ready communication | Customer waits until staff manually call or notify them. | Customers may be uncertain about when their order is ready. | Barista marks the order as ready and the customer receives an order-ready notification. | Improves customer waiting experience and service clarity. |
| Reorder management | Manager identifies low stock through manual checking or delayed stock review. | Reordering may happen too late, increasing stockout risk. | Inventory system checks reorder level after inventory deduction and generates low-stock alerts. | Supports proactive replenishment and reduces stockout risk. |
| Manager decision-making | Manager relies on manual observation and experience. | Decisions are reactive and less data-driven. | Manager reviews system-generated reorder requests before approving purchase orders. | Improves control, accountability, and data-based decision-making. |

## Summary of Key Improvements

| Key Improvement | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Real-time inventory checking | Prevents unavailable items from being confirmed. |
| Structured POS order capture | Reduces manual order entry errors. |
| Alternative item recommendation | Makes out-of-stock handling more efficient. |
| Automatic inventory deduction | Keeps stock data more accurate and up to date. |
| Low-stock alert and reorder request | Helps the manager reorder before stock runs out. |
| Order-ready notification | Improves customer communication and service experience. |

## Short Analysis

The To-Be process improves the original coffee shop order workflow by introducing system-supported decision points and automated inventory control. In the As-Is process, order handling and inventory checking depend heavily on manual communication, which can lead to delayed stock updates, order errors, and poor visibility for managers. The redesigned To-Be process shifts key activities into the POS and inventory system, including real-time availability checks, structured order capture, automatic stock deduction, low-stock alerts, and reorder request generation.

This redesign improves the process by reducing manual work, making exception handling more consistent, and giving the manager better visibility over inventory levels. The main business value is not only faster order processing, but also more reliable data for operational decision-making.
